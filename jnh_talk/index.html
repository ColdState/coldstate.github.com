Don't Look Back is an indie game by Terry Cavanagh based on the Orpheus and Euridice story. There's a lot of platforming, to rapidly get a sense of the whole game you might watch a playthrough video.

Midas is a entry in the LD48 #22 ("alone") Jam that uses some of these techniques.

These are a few scraps regarding storytelling:

  • MZB sez "A likable character overcomes almost insuperable odds and by his or her own efforts achieves a worthwhile goal."
  • Imitating the structure of the fortunately/unfortunately joke is reasonable. You can connect Flow to fortunately....
  • True forking plots are exponential amounts of work. Galatea is an example.
  • Mostly, instead of actually forking, the plot offers a choice where both choices lead to the same outcome. (Magician's Choice)
  • Often, instead of forking-and-rejoining with progress on both sides, one of the forks is actually a failure/loopback path. It does lead to the same outcome, because the player can recover/restart.
  • Artistic content (backdrop paintings, cutscenes, voiceovers) is very expensive to create. Games dole out artistic content, gated with obstacles.

These are a few techniques for conserving art:

  • Limited palette
  • Limited size in pixels
  • Limited lighting or window size
  • Symbolic rather than representational depiction
  • Consistent stylized aesthetic
  • Use text
